Disorders:

Depression

Often characterized as a loss of interest or pleasure, general sadness, feelings of low guilt, difficulty falling asleep, exhaustion and a loss of concentation. It affects over 300 million people worldwide. Depression doesn’t just arise as a result of “too much” or “too few” brain chemicals, specifically serotonin, as it is often depicted. Rather, several forces such as genetics, life events, medical problems and medications can bring the illness on.

Anxiety

Excessive nervousness, fear, apprehension, and worry. It can develop from a multitude of factors including genetics, brain chemical imbalance, and life events.

Bipolar Affective Disorder

Causes manic and depressive episodes, sometimes featuring moments of normal or stabilized mood, hyperactivity, inflated self-esteem, and a lack of desire to sleep. Affects about 60 million people worldwide. The real stimulus is not not entirely known, but it has shown to be a cause of a mixture of genetic, neurochemical, and evnironmental factors.

Schizophrenia

Characterized by distortions in thinking, perception, emotions, sense of self, and behavior. People diagnosed with schizophrenia experience hallucinations and delusions starting early adulthood. About 50 million people are impacted by this illness worldwide.

Dementia

A decline in cognitive functions including memory, orientation, thinking, comprehension, calculation, and language. It is caused by a vairety of disease that impact the brain. While there are no current cures there are several treatments designed to ease the suffering. It impacts over 50 million people around the globe.
